**august triptych**

 **I.**

Freedom is the fence gone slack,  
but the horses do not run.  
They stand in their own shadows,  
tails switching at summer flies,  
choosing, for now,  
to stay.

 **II.**

Seventeen years underground,  
the cicadas rise.  
Six weeks to sing  
themselves to death,  
a choir of urgency  
that makes me think  
of all I've buried,  
waiting for light  
that may never break.

 **III.**

The fireflies  
know what I forgot:

brief  
can be  
enough,  
  
even the darkness  
learns to hold light.
