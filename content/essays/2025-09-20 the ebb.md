# the ebb

## a poem to pause


[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a6LH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F34bab773-234a-446b-8ed4-7983427baefc_4284x2243.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a6LH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F34bab773-234a-446b-8ed4-7983427baefc_4284x2243.jpeg)

Do you ever think  
how the tide goes out  
each evening,  
not to abandon the shore,  
but to return saltier,  
fuller,  
alive with what it gathered.

Solitude is like that.  
A pulling back,  
a necessary hush,  
so that when I arrive again  
to the world of others,  
I arrive brimming.
